FOOD WASTE

What is Food Waste?

Food waste refers to food that is discarded, lost, or uneaten. It occurs at various stages of the food supply chain, from production and processing to retail and consumption. Globally, about one-third of all food produced is wasted, representing a significant economic, environmental, and social challenge.

Environmental Impact

Food waste in landfills generates methane, a potent greenhouse gas. Additionally, wasted food represents wasted resources used in production, including water, land, energy, labor, and capital. Reducing food waste is essential for sustainable resource management and climate change mitigation.

Sustainable Solutions

Anaerobic Digestion Food waste can be processed in anaerobic digesters to produce biogas for energy generation and nutrient-rich digestate for soil amendment.
Composting Composting food waste creates valuable soil amendments that enhance soil structure, fertility, and water retention capacity.
Animal Feed Certain types of food waste can be processed and used as ingredients in animal feed, reducing the need for conventional feed resources.
Value-Added Products Food waste can be transformed into specialty products like essential oils, pigments, enzymes, and other valuable biochemicals through biorefining processes.
